My Story

The journey that led me to Steam Dreams America

The Blackfoot Principle

The Blackfoot people understood something profound about leadership and community. Their name, derived from the blackened soles of their moccasins, speaks to a people who walked the earth with purpose, leaving their mark through steady, grounded steps. They built their confederacy not through division, but through unity of purpose.

This is the principle I bring to Steam Dreams America. Like the Blackfoot, I believe in standing firmly on the ground we know - the ground of American values, bipartisan cooperation, and the dignity of work. We don't need to tear down what works; we need to build upon it, together.

From Germany to America's Future

Currently studying in Germany, I'm learning from a nation that rebuilt itself through cooperation and innovation. But my heart remains in America, where I see the potential for something even greater - a renewal that doesn't require us to choose between progress and tradition, between left and right, between urban and rural.

The funds raised through this movement support my studies abroad, allowing me to bring back the best practices and insights to help America grow the right way. This isn't about personal gain; it's about investing in America's future through education and cross-cultural understanding.
